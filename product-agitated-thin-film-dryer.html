Product Overview

The Gausin Agitated Thin Film Dryer (ATFD) is a vertical, jacketed cylinder fitted with a rotor carrying hinged or fixed blades. The rotor spreads incoming feed into a thin, highly turbulent film over the heated wall, achieving fast evaporation even for difficult products.

Because the film is constantly wiped and renewed, the ATFD handles viscous, fouling, foaming and heat-sensitive feeds that would scale or burn in conventional dryers. Operation under vacuum lowers the boiling point and protects product quality.

ATFDs are a key element of Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) plants, converting evaporator concentrate into a dry, handleable powder or flakes. Gausin supplies them in SS 304 / 316L with optional crystallizing and condensing systems.

How It Works

1
Feed Entry
Concentrated feed is metered into the top of the heated cylinder.
2
Film Formation
Rotor blades spread the feed into a thin, turbulent film on the wall.
3
Evaporation
Heat from the jacket evaporates moisture / solvent from the moving film.
4
Vapour Removal
Vapour is drawn off under vacuum to a condenser for recovery.
5
Drying to Solid
As the film travels down, it dries into powder, flakes or cake.
6
Discharge
Dry solid is discharged via a screw or rotary valve at the base.
