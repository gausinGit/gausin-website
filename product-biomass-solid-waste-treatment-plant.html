Product Overview

The Gausin Biomass Solid Waste Treatment Plant converts agricultural residue, industrial solid waste, and organic rejects into usable outputs such as RDF, compost, or biomass fuel through integrated mechanical processing.

Plants include receiving, sorting, shredding, drying, baling or compaction, and emission control — sized for sugar mills, paper plants, food processors, and municipal solid waste facilities.

Gausin provides complete engineering from feed characterization and layout design through fabrication, erection, commissioning, and operator training.

How It Works

1
Receiving & Sorting
Waste is weighed, inspected, and sorted to remove inerts and recyclables.
2
Shredding
Primary and secondary shredders reduce particle size for uniform downstream processing.
3
Drying / Compaction
Thermal drying or baling produces stable RDF, briquettes, or compost feed.
4
Product Recovery
Finished RDF, biomass fuel, or compost is stored for dispatch or co-processing.
5
Emission Control
Dust collectors and scrubbers treat exhaust before stack release.
